Benefits of Studying in Canada

  1. Affordable Education:

    When compared to other English-speaking countries, studying in Canada is quite affordable.

  2. Canadian Education System:

    Canada provides limitless opportunities and a wide variety of courses with an inbuilt flexibility to choose pathways that serve their long term career goals. University programmes are organised through traditional lectures, combined with workshops, group work, assignments and projects. They guide you to become independent learners.

  3. Safe environment:

    The Canadian Government plays a pro-active role in ensuring that international students who come to Canada for their studies have legal protection. Canada is considered to be one of the safest counties in the world with low crime rate and a high quality of life.

  4. Promising Future:

    Canadians place great importance on learning and have developed a first- rate education system with high standards. The country spends more on education (as a percentage of GDP) compared to the OECD average, and is the second highest among G-8 countries.

  5. Diversity and Cosmopolitan Environment:

    Canada has a highly diverse population, resulting in a rich and vibrant cultural scene. You will get to experience a multicultural environment surrounding your classrooms.

Canada has become a booming hub for international students who aspires to study abroad. Being such an immense The universities and colleges in Canada always maintain high standards of education and the Canadian degrees are widely recognized worldwide making it easier for International students to gain employment soon after graduation.


Education in Canada is generally governed by the provinces.The universities and colleges in the country are respected for their quality of education. Generally colleges focus on providing diploma, post graduate diploma programs. Whereas the universities emphasis on bachelors, masters and doctoral degrees. Tuition fees and cost of living for international students is a bit relieved too.


STUDY SYSTEM

  1. Post Secondary Certificate:

    It can be done after class 12th. It is usually of one year duration.

  2. Diploma/ Advanced Diploma:

    Can either be done after Class 12th or Diploma after 10th in India. They provide an option of co-op work term which gives the student an opportunity to gain practical experience. These work terms can be paid or unpaid. The duration of the programs would be 2 or 3 years.

  3. Bachelor’s Degree:

    Bachelor Degrees are awarded by Universities/ University Colleges/ Community Colleges after 3 or 4 years of full-time study and can lead to graduate level studies.

  4. Fast Track/ Accelerated Programs:

    Are meant for Bachelor’s Degree or Diploma holders and the duration could be from 1-2 years in a specialized skill aimed at employability.

  5. Post Graduate Diplomas/ Certificates:

    The prerequisites to enroll in these programs are a Bachelor’s Degree or an Undergraduate Diploma.

  6. Masters Degree:

    It is an optional for Bachelors Degree holders, graduates who have an interest in further learning and research and those who want to broaden their career possibilities. It is for 1 or 2 years of duration and can be pursued after 4 years of Bachelor’s degree.

  7. Doctorate or PhD:

    PhD is a degree ranking above the Masters Degree and generally requires 3 to 7 years to complete.

Living In Canada


One of the most important factors to consider when studying abroad will be your budget. As a student you need to keep a track on all your expenses. Here is the overview of total estimate of living costs in Canada excluding your tuition fees:


  • Accomodation ( Depends on where you reside)
    CAD $600-$1200 per month
  • Utilities* (Only when not included with the rent)
    CAD $150-$200 per month
  • Groceries
    CAD $100-$150 per month
  • Internet and Phone
    CAD $80-$100 per month
  • Entertainment
    CAD $120-$150 per month
  • Transportation
    CAD $120-$140 per month


Working while studying is one of the many reasons Canada has become a favourable study destination for International students.

You may be allowed to work on or off campus depending on your study permit. Foreign students who have been issued a study permit to study at a publicly funded institution are allowed to work off campus while studying. To work off-campus, you do not have to apply for a work permit. The study permit authorizes you to work up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions, and full time during scheduled breaks.

The post-graduation work program is designed to provide students with Canadian work experience in their field of study. This program allows certain students to work for up to three years after their graduation. Students completing a two year diploma or a four year degree program would be eligible for a three years work permit.
