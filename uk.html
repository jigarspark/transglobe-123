Why Study In UK ?


Get to Know the Country

The United Kingdom, popularly known as the UK comprises the historic countries of England, Wales and Scotland, as well as Northern Ireland. With a population of around 66 million people, the UK remains as an economic and military power with great political and cultural influence around the globe.


Since years, the country has been a home to several migrants looking for a new start. It is considered to be one of the best places to live, work and maintain a high quality of life.

The United Kingdom is definitely considered as an educational hub for students around the globe. Whether it is a fast-paced life of London or a laid back culture of Edinburgh, you will definitely fall in love with the United Kingdom. Here are the few benefits of studying in the United Kingdom:


Benefits of Studying in the USA

  1. Degree Quality Assurance::

    UK universities are investigated frequently to guarantee that they maintain the standard expectations of educating, learning and research set by the Government.

  2. Home to the world’s greatest Universities:

    UK has always been one of the favourable study destinations for International Students. The country is home to some of the world’s renowned universities such as University of Oxford, University of Cambridge, Imperial College and more.

  3. Free healthcare benefits:

    The country has a national health system known as National Health Service (NHS). If you are a TIER-4 visa student enrolled in a full time course and studying for 6 months or more, you have access to the NHS.

  4. Scholarships:

    Financing your international education can definitely be hard. However, it's always a good idea to research for scholarships before you apply.

  5. Getaway to Europe:

    Being in the UK is of a great advantage as it allows you to easily travel around Europe. With access to Channel Tunnel and low cost airlines, weekend getaway will be very easy and exciting.

Plenty of courses are available in the British Universities making the country one of the world’s most popular study destinations for higher education. The biggest advantage of the UK educational system in comparison to other educational destinations worldwide provides shorter duration programs.

Living In UK


The cost of studying an undergraduate degree in the UK is generally higher compared to studying a postgraduate degree. In general the average tuition fees for pursuing an Undergraduate degree for overseas students is between 10000GBP – 15000 GBP. This varies from the university you have selected, the course and the location.Below table shows the approximate expenses for an International student living in the UK.


  • Accomodation ( Depends on where you reside)
    GBP 400-700 per month
  • Utilities* (Only when not included with the rent)
    GBP 40-50 per month
  • Groceries
    GBP 150-250 per month
  • Internet and Phone
    GBP 20-50 per month
  • Transportation
    GBP 50-70 per month
  • Entertainment
    GBP $150 per month


Being one of the most popular study destinations, UK attracts a large proportion of international students. Following are the generic set rules if you are on your Tier-4 visa:

  • Maximum of 20 hours per week for those studying at a degree level
  • Full-time work is allowed during vacations
  • Self-employment is not permitted, like any freelance or consultancy work
  • Also. before you start work, You will need to have applied for a National Insurance number to be eligible to work in the UK,

During the course of your study, if your syllabus demands a course of work, then there is a fixed time- limit you should work.


The UK has always been an attractive study destination for Indian students, but staying in the UK after you finish your course can be difficult. In view of the recent developments, students who are now enrolled for courses starting 2020/21 can stay back for two years after the completion of their course. Every UK University also has a career services team who guide students on where to apply for the right job, preparing your resume and also provide you with tips to ace the interview.
