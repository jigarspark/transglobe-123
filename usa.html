Why Study In USA ?


Get to Know the Country

With a surface area of a little under 10 million square kilometres, USA is one of the largest countries in the world. The country is divided into 50 breathtaking states and has a population of over 400 million people.


If you are planning to live, learn and grow – the United States is a place to be. Home to the venerable Ivy League, the United States has plenty of top-ranked universities. From the perspective of an International Student, if you want to experience a great sense of adventure, then US is the place to be.

It is one the most sought after study destinations. With thousands of academic programs, world-class institutions, and unmatched flexibility, the country offers a wealth of higher-education opportunities that you will not find anywhere else in the world. Some of the key benefits include:


Benefits of Studying in the USA

  1. Experience the Diversity:

    No other country can compete with the diversity of programs offered by universities and colleges in the USA.Depending on the experience you want, you can also consider the university’s location, courses, tuition fees, faculties, facilities and more.

  2. Range of Educational Choices:

    Canada provides limitless opportunities and a wide variety of courses with an inbuilt flexibility to choose pathways that serve their long term career goals. University programmes are organised through traditional lectures, combined with workshops, group work, assignments and projects. They guide you to become independent learners.

  3. Career Advancement:

    Universities and colleges in the US allows you to gain valuable work experience while studying as a way to kickstart your professional career. You may have an opportunity to obtain an internship during your time at the university.

  4. Multiple scholarships opportunities:

    A range of scholarship options available if you study in US.Almost all universities shall have scholarship programs open for international students, you will need to appear for certain exams and clear the eligibility criteria.

  5. Vibrant Campus Life:

    You will find yourself in the midst of new cultural experiences and the American way of life. Students can join a club, become a leader in student councils, write for the university newsletters or pursue their hobbies.

It is no surprise that out of the total student enrolment of 19 million in higher education, International students comprise almost 1 million, which is over 5 % of the student population. The degrees offered in the US are recognised worldwide for its academic excellence and the enhanced learning experience.


The higher education institutions in the US are not centrally managed but are accredited on a national or regional level by independent accrediting bodies. The colleges and universities basically offers three basic programs:

  • Associate’s Degree – An academic program taken by students after secondary school, prior to a Bachelors course.
  • Undergraduate Degree – The duration of undergraduate programs in the US is four years.
  • Postgraduate Degree – Master degrees in the US vary from one to two years.

ENTRY REQUIREMENTS


For an Undergraduate Degree:

  • Successful completion of the international equivalent of US/Canadian secondary education (Year 12)
  • Diploma or acceptable certification of graduation from High School in your home country
  • English language ability :
    1. TOEFL iBT: 80+
    2. IELTS 6+ bands
    3. SAT 1100+

For a Postgraduate Degree:

  • International equivalent of a North American four-year Bachelor’s degree program from an accredited university
  • If you have a three-year Bachelor’s degree or diploma, you can complete a qualifying Pre-Master’s program firsty
  • Official transcripts for all colleges/universities attended
  • English language ability :
    1. TOEFL-iBT 65- 80
    2. IELTS 6.5-7.0
    3. GMAT score 500+ /GRE score 290+

Living In USA


US definitely has some of the world’s best education institutions that attract a large number of international students every year. However, this also comes with high living costs. The living costs in the USA varies depending on the area you live in which is due to the vast territory of the country.The general monthly expenditure is as follows:


  • Accomodation ( Depends on where you reside)
    USD $700-$1500 per month
  • Utilities* (Only when not included with the rent)
    USD $150-$200 per month
  • Groceries
    USD $150-$200 per month
  • Internet and Phone
    USD $50-$100 per month
  • Transportation
    USD $70-$100 per month
  • Entertainment
    USD $150-$200 per month


Being an International student, the job hunting process can sometimes become a bit difficult but it’s always good to plan ahead. You must keep a track of rules and regulations that affect your employment status in the United States – both as a graduate and after graduation.

You can work for a maximum of 20 hours per week on-campus while the university is in session, and 40 hours per week during vacation period. Once you have completed your one full academic year, you can look for off-campus employment upon authorization by the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). There are basically 3 options for off-campus employment:

  • Curricular Practical Training (CPT)
  • Optional Practical Training (OPT)
  • Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Optional Practical Training Extension (OPT)

Another best way to find your future employer is to begin networking early. Around 70% of jobs are filled through solid connections. Take advantage of your college community and talk to alumni groups who have gone through the same processes.
